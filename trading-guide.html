Part 1: The Concepts

Before executing trades, you need to understand the building blocks. This section covers the "why" behind every setup.

1.1 The Two Types of Liquidity

Everything in this system revolves around two types of liquidity. Understanding this is the foundation of SMC trading.

Type What It Is Examples Role
External Liquidity Swing highs/lows where stop losses cluster PDH, PDL, overnight high/low, swing highs/lows TARGETS - where price is drawn to
Internal Liquidity Imbalance zones price uses as fuel FVGs, Order Blocks ENTRIES - where you take trades
The Core Principle: Price moves FROM internal liquidity TO external liquidity. Your job: Enter at internal liquidity (FVG/OB), exit at external liquidity (PDH/PDL/swing).
Visual: How Liquidity Drives Price
1.2 Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)

A Fair Value Gap is a 3-candle pattern where the wicks of candle 1 and candle 3 don't overlap, leaving an imbalance zone. The market "wants" to fill these gaps - they act as magnets and entry zones.

FVG Formation: Bullish vs Bearish

The 7 FVG Rules

1. Align with Order Flow
Only use bullish FVGs in uptrends, bearish FVGs in downtrends. Never trade counter to the current order flow.
2. Never Use Mitigated FVGs
Once price touches an FVG (even barely), it's weakened. Only trade fresh, untouched gaps.
3. Target Discount/Premium Zones
For longs: target FVGs in the lower half (discount) of the move. For shorts: target FVGs in the upper half (premium).
4. Stack Timeframes
Find a 1H or 4H FVG, then drop to 15m or 5m to find a smaller FVG inside it. This gives precise entries with HTF confluence.
5. Check the 3rd Candle
If 3rd candle continues in direction = "breakaway gap" (strong). If 3rd candle reverses = "rejection gap" (weak, avoid).
6. FVGs at Runs are Strongest
If an FVG forms during or right after a liquidity run (breakout through a key level), it's highest probability.
7. Avoid Consolidation FVGs
If FVGs keep failing both ways, you're in chop. Wait for two consecutive FVGs to be respected before trading again.

1.3 Order Blocks (OBs)

An Order Block is the last opposing candle(s) before an impulsive move. It represents where institutions placed their orders - and where they'll defend price if it returns.

Order Block Formation

Order Block Rules

Must Be Validated by BOS
It's not an OB until price breaks the highest wick (bullish) or lowest wick (bearish). Before that, it's just candles.
Use Candle Bodies, Not Wicks
Mark from open of first candle to close of last candle in the OB sequence. The bodies define the zone.
Candles Must Be Sequential
No opposing colored candles in between. If a bullish candle interrupts bearish ones, the sequence is broken.
Only Use Unmitigated OBs
Once price touches an OB, it's "used up." The orders have been filled. Never trade the same OB twice.
50% Level is Strongest
The midpoint of the OB typically sees the strongest reactions. Consider this your optimal entry point within the OB.
Must Form From a PDA
High-probability OBs form after sweeps or FVG rejections. Random OBs without context are lower probability.
The Golden Zone: OB + FVG Overlap

When an Order Block has a Fair Value Gap overlapping it, you have the highest probability entry zone possible. This is where institutions have both unfilled orders AND an imbalance to fill. Prioritize these setups above all others.

1.4 Market Structure: BOS and CHoCH

Understanding structure tells you which direction to trade. Two key concepts determine trend state:

BOS Break of Structure

Price breaks a swing high (bullish) or swing low (bearish), confirming trend continuation. This validates order blocks and tells you the trend is intact.

CHoCH Change of Character

The first break against the current trend. Signals potential reversal. Not confirmation yet - just a warning that things may be changing.

The Reversal Sequence: Sweep → CHoCH → BOS → Entry
The Reversal Confirmation Sequence:
  1. Sweep - Liquidity grabbed at key level (wick through, close back)
  2. CHoCH - First break against trend (warning signal)
  3. BOS - Price continues past CHoCH (trend officially reversed)
  4. Entry - First FVG/OB after BOS (the "Silver Bullet")
Don't Jump the Gun: Many traders enter right after the sweep. That's gambling. Wait for CHoCH + BOS to confirm the reversal is real. The "Silver Bullet" FVG after BOS is your highest probability entry.
1.5 Runs vs Sweeps

When price attacks external liquidity (a key level), it does one of two things. Knowing which is happening determines whether you join or fade the move.

Outcome What Happens How to Identify Your Trade
RUN Price breaks through and continues FVG forms DURING or AFTER breakout JOIN the move on pullback to FVG
SWEEP Price breaks through and reverses No FVG at breakout, or wick rejection FADE the move after CHoCH + BOS
Run vs Sweep Identification
Pre-Trade Assessment: Before price hits a key level, note where the last FVG formed. If it's BELOW the level being attacked (for a move up), that's a sign the move lacks fuel - expect a sweep. If price creates a NEW FVG at/after the breakout, it's likely a run.
1.6 The Opening Range (OR)

The Opening Range is the high and low of the first candle after 9:30 AM open. Most traders use the 15-minute OR for the best balance of reliability and range size.

Opening Range Setup

OR Trading Scenarios

TRADE Clean Breakout + FVG

Price closes above/below OR level. FVG forms at the breakout. Wait for pullback to FVG, then enter in breakout direction.

TRADE False Breakout + Opposing FVG

Price wicks through OR level but closes back inside. Opposing FVG forms. Enter on retest of FVG to fade the failed breakout.

CAUTION Breakout into HTF Level

OR breakout runs directly into PDH/PDL. High chance of reversal at that level. Either skip or take quick profits.

SKIP Wide OR (>40 pts) or Chop

If OR is wider than 40 points or price is chopping inside with no clear direction, skip the session. No edge.

Critical OR Rule: Always check what's above/below the OR before trading the breakout. If the OR high is 5 points below PDH, a long breakout will immediately run into major resistance. The OR is a tool for timing entries - higher timeframe levels always take priority.
1.7 Higher Timeframe Bias

Before every session, check the 1-hour chart to determine which direction the market wants to go. This takes 60 seconds and dramatically improves your win rate.

1
Find the Last 1H Swing High and Low
Look at the past 2-3 days of 1H candles. Identify the most recent significant swing high and swing low.
2
Which One Did Price Break Most Recently?
If price broke the swing high → HTF bias is BULLISH. If price broke the swing low → HTF bias is BEARISH.
3
Locate 1H FVGs/OBs
Mark any unmitigated 1H FVGs or Order Blocks. These are zones where price is likely to react during your session.

How HTF Bias Affects Your Trades

HTF Bias Sweep of Lows Sweep of Highs
Bullish A+ long setup (aligned with HTF) Lower probability (fading HTF trend)
Bearish Lower probability (fading HTF trend) A+ short setup (aligned with HTF)
Counter-HTF Trades: You CAN still take trades against the HTF bias, but require more confirmation (full CHoCH + BOS sequence) and consider sizing down. The best trades are when your setup aligns with the 1H direction.

2.3 Entry Criteria Checklist

Before entering ANY trade, confirm all of these:

HTF bias identified - You know if 1H is bullish or bearish
Trade aligns with HTF bias - Or has extra confirmation if counter-trend
Entry is at FVG or OB - You're entering at internal liquidity, not randomly
FVG/OB is unmitigated - It hasn't been touched before
Zone is in discount (longs) or premium (shorts) - Below/above 50% of the move
Clear target at external liquidity - You know where you're taking profit
Risk:Reward is at least 2:1 - Don't take trades with poor R:R
A+ Setup Qualifiers (Highest Probability)

These additional factors elevate a good setup to exceptional:

  • OB + FVG overlap ("Golden Zone")
  • Multi-timeframe confluence (1H FVG with 5m FVG inside)
  • Formed from a respected PDA (after sweep or FVG rejection)
  • Aligns perfectly with HTF bias
2.4 Entry, Stop, Target
ENTRY
  • Set limit order inside FVG/OB (typically at 50% of zone)
  • OR wait for price to enter zone and show rejection candle
  • For sweeps: enter on first FVG after BOS confirms ("Silver Bullet")
STOP LOSS
  • Beyond the FVG/OB (the invalidation point)
  • For sweeps: beyond the sweep wick
  • Typical range: 15-30 points on NQ
  • Never more than $400 risk per trade (MFF rules)
TARGET
  • Next external liquidity level (opposing PDH/PDL, swing high/low)
  • Or minimum 2:1 R:R
  • Consider: partials at 1:1, runner to full target
  • For MFF: aim for $400-600/day (consistency rule)
