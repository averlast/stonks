01 The core idea

Trade the Opening Range Breakout (first 15 min) early, then switch to Initial Balance (first 60 min) setups once the IB forms (~10:30 ET). Every trade must sit at a key level, wait for confirmation, and go with the higher-timeframe trend. The edge isn't the pattern — it's context + confluence + patience.

One sentence: Mark my levels before the open, wait for price to come to them, take the trade only when volume + candles confirm and the bigger trend agrees — and grade myself on whether I followed that, not on whether it won.

02 The levels I mark

Two kinds. Pre-session levels exist before 9:30 — I mark these blind in prep. Intraday levels form live during the session.

Pre-session (mark before the open)

  • High Volume Nodes — from the HTF visible-range volume profile; act as S/R.
  • Supply / Demand zones — the candle before an aggressive opposite-color engulfing candle (30m–4h).
  • PDH / PDL, prior-week & prior-month H/L.
  • Value Areas — VAH / VAL / POC for prior session and prior week.
  • Session liquidity — Asia (Tokyo) H/L, London H/L. Pre-market low ≈ London low.
  • VWAP — anchored at NY-open, daily-open, and weekly.

Intraday (form live)

  • Opening Range — high/low of the first 15 min + the 50% midpoint.
  • Initial Balance — high/low of the first 60 min + 25/50/75% + fib extensions (targets).
  • Developing VWAP — updates every bar.
Confluence rule: when 2–3 of these stack at the same price (e.g. OR high + a HVN + weekly VWAP), the trade is far higher probability. Stack, then wait.

04 The setups

Tap a card to expand. ORB setups run early; IB setups after the hour; the liquidity-sweep reversal is the bread-and-butter that overlays everything.

ORB — 3 ways to trade the 15-min range

1 · Breakout & retest (continuation)

Price breaks the OR, then retests the broken edge → enter the continuation.

wait for 5m close outside the range

2 · Range trade

Bounce off one side of the range back toward the other. Best when the break was a fake-out / liquidity grab that failed.

target the opposite side

3 · Retracement continuation favorite

After a clean breakout, wait for a deeper pull to the 50% of the range or VWAP (A+ when both line up), then enter the continuation.

50% + VWAP confluence = A+
Timing tell: break within 20–25 min = momentum day → trade breakouts off the range. Takes >30 min to break = choppy/range day → wait for the deeper 50%/VWAP retrace.
IB — 3 ways to trade the first-hour range

1 · Breakout & retest (continuation)

Break of IB high/low, retest, continue. Direction bias from which side broke first.

2 · Failed auction + liquidity sweep high win rate

Price poked outside the IB then closes back inside (30-min candle by definition; read early on 1m/5m). Fade it back toward IB mid. Strongest when it also swept a session level.

target IB mid (50%), then range edge

3 · Retracement continuation

After a break, enter the pullback to the 25% or 50% retracement, continue toward the extension.

Liquidity-sweep reversal — the bread & butter (3-step filter)
Step 1
Key level. Only at a marked level (PDH/PDL, session H/L, value area, S/D) — never a random 1m wick.
Step 2
Confirmation. A strong wick through the level + a volume increase + an engulfing candle back in my direction.
Step 3
With the greater trend. 1h/4h agreeing → hold for the home run. Against it → scalp only.

Mechanic: price wicks through the level to trip stops, then sharply reverses. Stop below the wick; target VWAP / range mid; move to break-even fast.

Double liquidity sweep — my A+ (~85% win rate when it appears)

Two coincident levels swept at once — e.g. IB low + London low just below each other, or ONH + IB high above. Price wicks below both, gets bought back up hard → failed auction. I take it every time it presents.

IB low + London lowONH + IB high
Supply / Demand rejection

Price trades into a drawn S/D zone (HTF). Don't take the first touch of a big zone — wait for a 5m engulfing rejection + retest inside the zone, then enter with a tight stop past the zone.

S/D zones often consolidate/trap rather than sweep — different confirmation than a liquidity level.

05 The confirmation gate (don't catch a falling knife)

Charts live on the 5-minute to stay unemotional; I drop to the 1-minute only as price nears a key level to time the entry. The 15-minute holds the Opening Range.

Hard rule: never trade the immediate first break. If it hasn't closed beyond the level with volume, it isn't confirmed — it's bait.

07 Risk & trade management

Stops & size

  • Stop below the wick / below the engulfing candle — give a little room.
  • Size so a full stop is small $$ (an 11-pt NQ stop is a "paper cut" if sized right).
  • Consistent size — erratic sizing is an execution red flag.

Targets & scaling

  • Scale out: TP1 at 25/50% or IB mid, TP2 at the range edge, runner to LOD/HOD.
  • Scale in: add on the confirmation retest.
  • Trail and move to break-even aggressively once it works.
R, anchored to the first entry. 1R = initial size × initial-stop distance × $/pt. Total R = realized $ ÷ 1R. Adding size later does not change what 1R means. A clean plan that still stops out = a paid-for, correct loss.

08 Bias & data (trade with the odds)

Call the day up front — bull / bear / chop — informed by base rates computed from history:

  • Opening-candle continuation — a green first-60-min candle → the session tends to close green (and vice versa).
  • IB break — usually only one side of the IB breaks on the day.
  • IB retracement depth — on a breakout day, how far pullbacks typically reach (25% vs 50%).
  • IB extension hit-rates — how often each extension target is reached → sets realistic targets.
Read the % right. The specific numbers in the videos ("77%", "78%") are example-day figures, not constants — they change daily and by lookback. In my own tool they'll be computed as-of that day (no future data). The principle is what to memorize: trade with the data, not against it.

09 Mistakes that cost me (pre-flight avoid list)

  • Chasing the first break instead of waiting for the 5m close.
  • Trading every ORB / every 1m wick with no key level.
  • Oversizing because volatility is high.
  • Trading against the higher-timeframe trend.
  • Catching a falling knife — no volume/candle confirmation.
  • FOMO re-entry after missing a move.
